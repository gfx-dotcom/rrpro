What is a trading journal and why do I need one?
A trading journal is a tool to track every trade you make, including entry/exit prices, profit/loss, strategy used, and emotional state. You need one because traders who journal improve their win rate by 12% on average within 6 months by identifying patterns and mistakes.
Is TradeJournal free to use?

How do I calculate my risk-reward ratio?
Risk-Reward Ratio = (Take Profit - Entry Price) / (Entry Price - Stop Loss). For example, if you enter at $100, stop loss at $95, and take profit at $115, your R:R is 15/5 = 3:1. TradeJournal calculates this automatically for every trade.
What is the 2% rule in trading?
The 2% rule means never risking more than 2% of your total trading capital on a single trade. If you have $10,000, risk maximum $200 per trade. This protects you from account blowouts and allows you to survive 50 consecutive losses.
Can I use TradeJournal for forex, crypto, and stocks?
Absolutely! TradeJournal supports all markets: forex (EUR/USD, GBP/JPY, etc.), cryptocurrencies (Bitcoin, Ethereum), stocks, indices, and commodities. You can track any asset you trade.
How does TradeJournal help improve my trading performance?
TradeJournal provides analytics showing your most profitable pairs, best trading times, win rate trends, risk-reward performance, and emotional patterns. This data helps you identify what works and eliminate what doesn't.
Is my trading data private and secure?
Yes. All your data is stored locally in your browser using localStorage. We don't store your trades on any server. You have full control and can export or delete your data anytime.
What's the difference between win rate and risk-reward ratio?
Win rate is the percentage of trades you win (e.g., 60%). Risk-reward ratio is how much you make vs risk per trade (e.g., 1:3). A 40% win rate with 1:3 R:R is more profitable than 70% win rate with 1:1 R:R due to expectancy mathematics.
