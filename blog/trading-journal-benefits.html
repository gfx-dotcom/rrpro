üìù The 7 Golden Rules of Journaling

1Log Every Single Trade

2Track Your Emotional State

Note how you felt before, during, and after each trade. Were you anxious? Overconfident? This reveals patterns in emotional trading.

3Record Your Setup & Strategy

What was the technical setup? Which strategy did you follow? This lets you see which setups actually work for you.

4Document Screenshots

Save chart screenshots of your entries and exits. Visual learning is powerful‚Äîyou'll spot mistakes you didn't know you were making.

5Calculate Risk-Reward Ratios

Log your planned R:R and actual R:R. You might think you're trading 1:2, but the data could show you're actually hitting 1:1.

6Review Weekly, Not Daily

Obsessing over every trade daily leads to overtrading. Review your journal weekly to identify real patterns, not noise.

7Act on Insights

A journal is useless if you don't implement what you learn. If the data shows you lose money trading news events, stop trading news.
